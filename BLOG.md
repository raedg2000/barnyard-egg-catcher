# Barnyard Egg Catcher: Simple, Fast, and Family-Friendly

Barnyard Egg Catcher is a lighthearted browser game built around a very simple idea: chickens lay eggs, and the player has to move a farmer with a basket to catch them before they hit the ground. Each egg is worth 10 points, five broken eggs ends the run, and the pace gradually increases as the score goes up.

What makes the game work well is its focus on accessibility. The farmer snaps between five clear lanes, the controls are easy to learn with either keyboard or touch input, and a small catch-assist zone helps younger players stay close to the action even when their timing is not perfect. That keeps the game challenging without making it frustrating.

The project was also rebuilt with modern web tools. It uses TypeScript, Phaser 3, and Vite to create a cleaner structure, smoother animation, and an easier development workflow. Alongside the gameplay loop, the game includes a menu screen, pause support, floating score feedback, playful farm visuals, and audio that can use a local Old MacDonald-style track with a generated fallback.

Barnyard Egg Catcher is a good example of how a small idea can still feel polished. With clear rules, friendly visuals, and a difficulty curve that stays manageable, it is the kind of game that is easy to pick up, easy to share, and fun to replay.
